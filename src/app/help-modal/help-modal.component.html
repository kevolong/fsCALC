Modes

Footage -> Size: Estimate footage size based on length of footage in hours and minutes. Choose a codec, resolution, and framerate, in that order as resolution options are dependent on codec choice, and framerate options are dependent on resolution choice. Bitrate is set by the codec.

Size -> Footage: Estimate amount of footage that can fit a space size. Choose a codec, resolution, and framerate, in that order as resolution options are dependent on codec choice, and framerate options are dependent on resolution choice. Bitrate is set by the codec.

Custom Bitrate: Estimate footage size based on length of footage in hours and minutes. Enter a bitrate for use with codecs that have a user-defined bitrate, such as H.264 or H.265. Resolution and framerate do not apply.

Calculation Accuracy

Calculations are for video files only. Audio files are usually processed and stored separately. Some codecs may include camera audio, but many professional codecs (such as DNxHD and ProRes) do not. If you have camera or external audio files, they are not included in the calculations.

File sizes are rounded to two decimal points. Footage minutes are rounded down.

Calculations are a close estimate for codecs with a constant bitrate (CBR). Actual file sizes will vary slightly and may be higher or lower.

Calculations for codecs with a variable bitrate (VBR) are made at the maximum bitrate, so are over-estimates of varying degree depending on the complexity of the video.

If you don't know whether the codec you are using has a constant or variable bitrate, check out the codec links in the section below. Codecs with user-defined bitrates may be constant or variable.
